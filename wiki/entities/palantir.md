# Palantir Technologies

US defense and intelligence data analytics company. Co-founded by [[entities/alexander-karp]], Peter Thiel, and others (~2003). Core business: software platforms (Gotham, Foundry, AIP) for government intelligence, military operations, and commercial data analysis.

## Key facts

- Early investors/founders: Peter Thiel, Alex Karp, Joe Lonsdale, Stephen Cohen, Nathan Gettings
- Primary clients historically: US intelligence community (CIA, NSA), military, law enforcement
- Commercial platform (Foundry) expanded 2010s–2020s
- AIP (Artificial Intelligence Platform) launched 2023 — integrates LLMs into enterprise and government workflows; the AIP "bootcamp" GTM motion drove the 2024–2025 commercial-revenue surge
- Listed on NYSE (PLTR)

## Scale (FY2025 + Q1 2026, fact-checked 2026-07-09)

The abstract "Western-values defense-tech company" of 2023 became, by 2025–26, one of the most valuable software companies on Earth — the empirical backdrop that makes [[concepts/technological-republic]] more than a manifesto:

- **FY2025 revenue ≈ $4.475B**, +56% YoY (Q4 2025 alone +70% to ≈$1.41B) — verified vs Palantir 8-K earnings releases / Macrotrends
- **Q1 2026 (reported 2026-05-04):** revenue **$1.63B, +85% YoY** — the *fastest growth since the 2020 direct listing* and accelerating vs FY25's +56%; **US revenue $1.28B, +104% YoY** (first time US growth crossed 100%), split US-gov $687M (+84%) and US-commercial $595M (+133%); net income **$870.5M / $0.34 diluted EPS** (vs $214M / $0.08 a year prior, ~4×). FY2026 guidance **raised to $7.65–7.66B (~71% YoY)**, up from the ≈$7.18–7.20B guided in Feb 2026 and well above the ~$7.27B consensus.
- **The de-rating nuance (the interesting part):** despite this fundamental *acceleration*, the stock **fell ~27% YTD** in H1 2026 (≈$177.75 start-of-year → ≈$129.30 on 2026-07-02). Market cap peaked ≈$424B (Dec 2025), troughed with the sell-off, then re-crossed **$300B in early July 2026** (≈$309B on 2026-07-02 → ≈$322B on 2026-07-07). So 2026-H1 is a textbook *multiple-compression-into-accelerating-fundamentals* episode: the valuation ran ahead of even a company growing 85%, then the growth had to catch up to the price. This is the empirical caveat to the [[concepts/technological-republic]] "priced like a hyperscaler" read — the *business* re-coupling (§ below) is realer than the *stock* re-rating.
- **Maven Smart System (MSS):** US DoD raised the contract ceiling to **≈$1.3B through 2029** (May 2025); **NATO** signed a **sole-source** procurement of MSS NATO (Mar 2025, ~6-month cycle — among the fastest in NATO history). MSS is the production descendant of Project Maven, the 2017 DoD computer-vision program at the centre of Google's 2018 "Maven" employee revolt — the exact Silicon-Valley-refuses-defense episode Karp built a career criticising.

## Ideological positioning

Palantir is explicitly a Western-values-aligned defense-tech company. [[entities/alexander-karp]] and Peter Thiel have been vocal that building tools for Western governments is a moral obligation, not just a business:
- Palantir declined to work with certain foreign governments
- Karp publicly criticized Silicon Valley peers for refusing US defense contracts (Google's Maven controversy)

This philosophy is formalized in [[sources/technological-republic-karp-2025]].

## Relevance

The Technological Republic can be read partly as a philosophical rationale for Palantir's business model — critics note the self-serving element; defenders argue the thesis stands independently.
