# Raymond Lo

AI developer advocate and edge-AI engineer. Currently **Developer Advocate Manager, Robotics and Embedded Devices at [[entities/nvidia]]** (Oct 2025–present, based San Jose). Career arc spans wearable computing research → AR hardware company founder → cloud/AI evangelism at three platform vendors (Google, Samsung, Intel) → robotics/edge advocacy at NVIDIA.

## Background

- **PhD, Computer Engineering, University of Toronto** — advised by **Steve Mann** (widely credited as the father of wearable computing; MIT Media Lab '97). Decade-long research collaboration on GPGPU computing, open-source HDR video, and wearable computing prototypes.
- **2011** — with Mann, built the first Meta collaborative AR eyeglass prototype in Toronto.
- **2012** — co-founded **Meta** (the AR-glasses company, NOT to be confused with Facebook/Meta) alongside Ben Sand, Meron Gribetz, and Steve Mann. Y Combinator-backed.
- **2013–2018(ish)** — CTO of Meta. Shipped Meta 1 and Meta 2 AR headsets with a 150+ person team; the company raised $80M+ before later winding down operations.
- **Google Cloud AI** — Technical Solutions Consultant.
- **Samsung NEXT** — Technology Evangelist.
- **Intel** — Global Lead, AI Software Evangelist for **[[concepts/openvino|OpenVINO]]** (Intel's edge-inference optimization toolkit). Grew the OpenVINO notebooks repo to 1,000+ stars with 70+ demos; contributed Stable Diffusion v2 performance improvements.
- **NVIDIA** (Oct 2025–) — Developer Advocate Manager, Robotics and Embedded Devices. Focus: Jetson Orin, edge AI, robotics frameworks (LeRobot).
