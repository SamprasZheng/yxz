# Audrey Tang

Taiwanese civic technologist and former Digital Minister of Taiwan; co-author of *Plurality*, a book framing collaborative diversity as a governance philosophy.

## Key facts

- **Role:** Former Digital Minister of Taiwan; pioneer of civic-tech and participatory democracy (g0v movement, vTaiwan, Polis-based consultations)
- **Book:** Co-author of *Plurality* (open-source, [github.com/pluralitybook/plurality](https://github.com/pluralitybook/plurality)) — see [[concepts/plurality]]
- **Affiliation:** Collaborates with [[entities/project-liberty]] on decentralized social protocols and plural governance research

## Why she matters here

Tang's work bridges civic-tech OpenGov (transparency, citizen participation) and Web3 on-chain governance models such as [[entities/polkadot|Polkadot OpenGov]]. *Plurality* is the conceptual vocabulary used to compare these systems in [[sources/plurality-audrey-tang-sampras-2025]].
