# Project Liberty

A US **$500-million initiative** founded by real-estate/sports billionaire **Frank H. McCourt, Jr.** (via McCourt Global) to break the data-monopoly model of incumbent social platforms and give users portable identity and ownership of their social graph. Started **2019**, publicly launched **2021**. It is the primary sponsor of [[concepts/dsnp]] and of the [[entities/frequency]] blockchain that hosts DSNP data.

## Structure (layer-down)

Project Liberty is not a single company but a stack of vehicles:

| Vehicle | Role |
|---|---|
| **McCourt Global** | Funding source; Frank McCourt's family holding company |
| **Project Liberty Institute** | Non-profit steward of [[concepts/dsnp]] as **"digital public infrastructure"** — keeps the protocol free and open; hosts academic fellows (incl. **[[entities/audrey-tang]]**, Senior Fellow 2024–) and partners with university labs (Stanford, Georgetown, Sciences Po) |
| **Amplica Labs** | The for-build engineering arm; develops [[entities/frequency]] + DSNP core protocols and the MeWe migration; acquired the **Speakeasy** AI civil-dialogue platform (2024) |

## The People's Bid for TikTok — outcome (deal closed; verified 2026-07-05)

> **⚠️ Resolved — the deal has closed.** Project Liberty's **"People's Bid for TikTok"** (formal offer to ByteDance announced **2025-01-09**, ahead of the US divestiture deadline) proposed to rebuild TikTok US on DSNP/American-owned infrastructure. **The bid was not selected.** Following President Trump's **Executive Order of 2025-09-25**, TikTok US was reorganized into **TikTok USDS Joint Venture LLC**, and the deal **closed on 2026-01-22** (Bloomberg). Structure: new-investor consortium ~50% (Oracle, Silver Lake, and Abu Dhabi's MGX ~15% each), affiliates of existing ByteDance investors ~30.1%, ByteDance ~19.9%; former TikTok head of operations/trust-and-safety **Adam Presser** installed as CEO of the American venture. Project Liberty's vision of a user-owned TikTok did **not** prevail; the outcome instead concentrated control in a conventional US-investor consortium. This is the canonical "the user-owned-infra pitch lost to the incumbent-capital pitch" data point for the cluster.

The episode still mattered: it put "who owns the social graph" into mainstream US political discourse and stress-tested whether a DSNP-based migration of a 170 M-user app was credible.

## Six-region placement

Project Liberty is the **US** node of the user-owned-social movement — privately funded, founder-driven. Its peers per region (full map on [[synthesis/digital-democracy-user-owned-social-six-region]]): Europe = standards/regulation (W3C ActivityPub, eIDAS/EUDI); Taiwan = civic-tech ([[entities/audrey-tang]]); the rest consume rather than set the standard. The structural point: in the US the push is *philanthropic-private*; nowhere else is a single $500 M private fund the prime mover.

## Long-horizon view (scenario / projection)

Project Liberty's wager is that data portability becomes a *right* and the social graph becomes *public infrastructure* the way numbering portability became mandatory in telecoms. The TikTok-bid loss is the near-term evidence that capital and incumbency still beat the user-ownership thesis; the century question is whether regulation (EU-style) eventually forces what the market would not.
