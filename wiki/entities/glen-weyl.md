# E. Glen Weyl

US economist and social technologist; founder of the **RadicalxChange** movement and co-author with **[[entities/audrey-tang]]** of *Plurality* (2024). Originator (with collaborators) of the mechanism-design toolkit — **Quadratic Voting** and **Quadratic/Plural Funding** — that supplies the formal backbone of the [[concepts/plurality]] philosophy.

## Why he matters here

The wiki's [[concepts/plurality]] cluster previously cited only Audrey Tang; Weyl is the co-author and the source of its *quantitative* half. He is the intellectual link between three worlds: academic mechanism design, the Ethereum public-goods ecosystem (Vitalik Buterin, Gitcoin), and Taiwan civic-tech (Tang). Splitting him into his own node makes that bridge explicit instead of buried.

## Key contributions (layer-down)

| Work | Year | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| *Radical Markets* (with Eric Posner) | 2018 | Introduced **Quadratic Voting (QV)** and the "common ownership / Harberger tax" ideas to a wide audience |
| *"Liberal Radicalism"* (with Vitalik Buterin & Zoë Hitzig) | 2018 | The **Quadratic/Plural Funding (QF)** paper — the math behind **Gitcoin Grants** and most crypto public-goods matching |
| **RadicalxChange** | 2018– | Non-profit/movement deploying QV/QF and "plural" social-tech globally |
| *Plurality* (with [[entities/audrey-tang]]) | 2024-05-20 | Synthesizes deliberation tech + plural funding into a single governance philosophy |

He is described as a principal collaborator of Vitalik Buterin (Ethereum), Jaron Lanier, and Audrey Tang.

## Six-region note

Weyl is the **US**-side intellectual anchor of the plural-governance field; [[entities/audrey-tang]] is the Taiwan-side practitioner anchor. The QF mechanism has its largest live deployment in the (globally distributed, US-led) Ethereum/Gitcoin ecosystem; QV experiments have appeared in US state legislatures (Colorado) and European municipal budgeting.
