# Alexander C. Karp

CEO and co-founder of [[entities/palantir]]. Unusual background for a tech executive: PhD in **neoclassical social theory** from Goethe University Frankfurt (2002), in the Frankfurt School milieu. Known for outspoken, philosophically-inflected views on tech, defense, and Western civilization. Karp is the **living embodiment of the [[concepts/technological-republic]] thesis** — a philosopher running the West's most politically explicit defense-tech company — which is why the book reads to critics as a rationale for Palantir's own business (see [[concepts/technological-republic#critique-of-the-concept]]).

## Key facts

- BA Haverford College; JD Stanford Law School; **PhD neoclassical social theory, Goethe University Frankfurt, 2002**
- Doctoral dissertation *Aggression in der Lebenswelt* ("Aggression in the Lifeworld") extended Talcott Parsons's concept of aggression. He went to Frankfurt intending to study under **Jürgen Habermas**, but the two parted ways and sociologist **Karola Brede** ultimately supervised the thesis — so "studied under Habermas" is a *near-miss* often repeated uncritically; the Habermasian "communicative rationality" influence on [[concepts/soft-belief]] is real, the formal supervision is not.
- Co-founded Palantir Technologies with **Peter Thiel** and others (incorporated **2003**); CEO since founding
- Long-time public advocate for defense-tech collaboration; Palantir's core business is government/intelligence/military contracts (see [[entities/palantir]] for the 2025 financial scale)
- Academic background in social theory distinguishes his thinking from typical Silicon Valley product-focused CEOs
- Author of [[sources/technological-republic-karp-2025]] (with Nicholas W. Zamiska; Crown Currency, 18 Feb 2025; NYT #1)

## Intellectual positions (per [[sources/technological-republic-karp-2025]])

- Silicon Valley has abandoned its defense-tech origins for consumer trivialities ("light hedonism")
- Engineers have a patriotic duty to work on hard national challenges, not optimize ad clicks
- AI is the decisive battlefield of 21st-century great-power competition
- "Soft belief" — shared purpose and cultural conviction — is a prerequisite for civilization-scale achievement
- Advocates a "new Manhattan Project" for AI
