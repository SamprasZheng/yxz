# Ripple

## What it is

**Ripple** is a US enterprise blockchain / crypto-payments company (San Francisco) built around the **XRP Ledger (XRPL)** and, since December 2024, its regulated US-dollar stablecoin **RLUSD (Ripple USD)**. By mid-2026 Ripple is a first-class node in the agentic-payments graph on two axes at once: it joined the [[concepts/x402-protocol|x402]] settlement layer as a founding **Premier Member**, and it is one of the few crypto firms to clear the [[concepts/agentic-payment-protocols|MiCA]] bar in Europe. It sits at the intersection the six-region map cares about — a US-originated rail vendor that is also a fully-licensed European operator.

## Why it matters here

Until July 2026 the [[concepts/x402-protocol|x402]] rail was effectively a **USDC-on-Base/Solana** story ([[entities/coinbase|Coinbase]] + Circle). Ripple's entry broadens that to a second settlement token (RLUSD) and a non-EVM/non-Solana chain (the XRP Ledger), diluting the "x402 = USDC monoculture" read that the [[synthesis/agentic-payments-six-region]] map noted. It is the clearest sign that x402 is consolidating into *the* neutral settlement standard rather than a Coinbase house protocol.

## The RLUSD ↔ MiCA nuance (a sharper version of the "digital dollarization" story)

Ripple's July 2026 double-win hides an asymmetry that matters for the [[synthesis/agentic-payments-six-region|Europe row]]:

- The **CASP** license lets Ripple *operate* regulated crypto-payments **services** across the EEA.
- But under [[concepts/agentic-payment-protocols|MiCA]] a single-fiat-pegged stablecoin is an **e-money token (EMT)** and may only be publicly offered to EU investors if the issuer holds a **separate EMT authorisation**. As of July 2026 **RLUSD is *not* in ESMA's EMT register** — so a licensed Ripple still cannot passport its own stablecoin to EU retail the way [Circle's EURC/USDC](https://www.esma.europa.eu/) can.

This is the same structural fact driving the euro-stablecoin debate: MiCA's EMT rules are a high, **US-issuer-favouring** bar (Circle cleared it first and now holds ~41% of the euro-stablecoin market), and even a fully-CASP-licensed challenger like Ripple is gated at the token layer. It is why the EU opened a **MiCA stablecoin rewrite** in July 2026. See [[synthesis/agentic-payments-six-region]].
