# The Techno-Industrial State / Defense-Tech Alliance — Six-Region Map

**Canonical question:** *Who is rebuilding the public-private defense-tech compact, by region, and by what governance model?* This is the geopolitical/industrial-policy frame around [[concepts/technological-republic]] — Karp & Zamiska's [[sources/technological-republic-karp-2025]] argues the US must revive a Manhattan-Project-style fusion of frontier engineering talent and state purpose; this page situates that *US-branded manifesto* inside the worldwide reversion to techno-industrial statecraft that is happening simultaneously across all six regions by different rules.

Sibling of the other six-region maps ([[synthesis/open-weight-llm-agent-stack-six-region]], [[synthesis/space-regulatory-regimes-six-region]], [[synthesis/llm-satellite-operations-six-region]], [[synthesis/orbital-data-center-six-region]]). Where those map *layers* (models / spectrum / ops-AI / compute), this maps the **political-economy substrate** beneath them: the state↔frontier-tech compact that funds and directs all of them.

## The central thesis, restated for comparison

The 21st-century power competition is decided less by who *has* frontier AI/compute than by **who fuses it with state purpose fastest** — and the binding constraint Karp names is *cultural will* ([[concepts/soft-belief]]), not capital or chips. The comparative insight: every bloc is rebuilding this compact at once, but the **supply mechanism for "soft belief" differs** — organic / market-driven (US), state-mandated (China), threat-forged (Taiwan), or fractured (Europe). The US thesis is therefore not unique; it is the *late, commercial, ideologically-narrated* instance of a global pattern.

## Three governance models

1. **Market-led fusion (US)** — venture capital + commercial primes do what government labs once did; the state is customer, not builder. Fast, but soft belief is genuinely contested (the book exists *because* it is not guaranteed).
2. **State-mandated fusion (China)** — soft belief and resource direction are supplied by the party-state; abundant cohesion, structurally opaque, dual-use by law. The mirror-image the US thesis is racing.
3. **Threat-forged / federated (Taiwan, Japan, Korea, Europe)** — cohesion comes from proximity to an adversary (Taiwan, Japan) or industrial-export ambition (Korea), or is deliberately pooled-yet-fractured (Europe's sovereign autonomy across 27 states).

## The 100-year invariant (labeled scenario, not forecast)

The structural bet: **the 1995–2020 era of borderless consumer software was the anomaly; the long-run default is fused compute-and-state-power.** If true, AI/compute/defense stacks harden along the six lines above — coherent with the orbital-slot enclosure of [[synthesis/space-regulatory-regimes-six-region]] and the model-sovereignty split of [[synthesis/open-weight-llm-agent-stack-six-region]]. The unresolved century-wildcard is the book's own blind spot: **a model that mobilizes will via a permanent external threat is unstable across a century of shifting adversaries, and "republic" can quietly decay toward technocratic security-state.** Karp never answers *whose* republic governs the AI it wants built — that governance question is the falsifier of the optimistic case.
