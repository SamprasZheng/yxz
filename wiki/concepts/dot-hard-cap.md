# DOT Hard Supply Cap

A supply ceiling of **2.1 billion DOT** introduced via Polkadot governance in March 2026, marking a fundamental shift in DOT's monetary policy from inflationary to deflationary/capped.

## Key facts

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Hard cap | 2.1 billion DOT |
| Enacted | March 14, 2026 |
| Governance vehicle | Referendum 1710 |
| Approval | 81% |
| Issuance cut | ~53.6% annual reduction |

## Mechanism

Before the cap, DOT had an uncapped inflationary supply (originally 10% annual inflation, later reduced to linear model). Post-cap, issuance follows Bitcoin-style diminishing curves — new supply creation approaches zero as the 2.1B ceiling is reached.

Validator rewards are simultaneously capped at **$5,000/month** with a biennial halving, cutting projected security costs from ~$500M/year to ~$90M/year.

## Context & significance

Proposed by [[entities/gavin-wood]] at the 2025 Berlin Web3 Summit as part of the "Second Era" package. The cap addresses a long-standing community concern that perpetual inflation suppressed DOT price appreciation and undermined long-term holder confidence.

[[entities/alice-und-bob]] (Tommi Enenkel) reported on and helped shape the referendum. His public position: supply-side changes are necessary but should be paired with demand-side ecosystem growth.

## Comparison

- **Bitcoin**: 21M BTC hard cap; Polkadot's cap is 100× larger but follows same philosophical model
- **Ethereum**: No hard cap (though EIP-1559 creates deflationary pressure via burns)
- **Pre-cap DOT**: Inflationary, justified as staking incentive; criticized as diluting non-stakers
