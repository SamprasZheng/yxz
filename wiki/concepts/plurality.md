# Plurality

Governance philosophy — and the title of an open-source book co-authored by [[entities/audrey-tang]] — arguing that decision-making should be **plural**: multi-perspective, inclusive, and adaptive rather than driven by a single centralized authority.

## Core tenets

- Reject single-path decision-making; embrace **multi-angle deliberation**
- Build governance structures that are **inclusive and adaptive**
- Allow participants to explore **multiple possibilities** depending on context rather than being forced down one predetermined route
- Treat **collaborative diversity** as the operating principle of legitimate governance

## Two parallel implementations

[[sources/plurality-audrey-tang-sampras-2025]] frames two complementary expressions of the Plurality impulse:

1. **Civic-tech OpenGov** — transparency, civic participation, accountability between governments and citizens. Driven by Audrey Tang's lineage of work in Taiwan.
2. **On-chain OpenGov** — token-weighted, technology-driven governance as implemented by [[entities/polkadot]]. Self-upgrading, transparent by virtue of blockchain.

Both reject centralized governance but differ on emphasis: institutional reform vs. technical decentralization.
