# Coalition 2026 Cyber Claims Report (covering calendar year 2025)

## What This Source Is

Coalition is one of the largest cyber insurance providers in the US by premium volume. Their annual cyber claims report is based on first-party claims data from their policyholder base, making it a high-quality statistical sample of actual insured losses — distinct from FBI IC3 self-reported complaint data.

Coalition publishes two annual reports that analysts tend to conflate by year label:

- The **2025 Cyber Claims Report** (published May 7, 2025) covers **calendar year 2024** claims data.
- The **2026 Cyber Claims Report** (published March 5, 2026) covers **calendar year 2025** claims data.

## Key Interpretation: Diverging Demand vs. Ransom Economy

The 2026 report captures a structural shift: **ransom demands surged 47% but payment rates collapsed to 14%** (100% − 86% refusal). Attackers are escalating asks as a negotiating opener; defenders with good backup/IR plans routinely refuse. This makes the "average ransom demand" metric a poor proxy for actual financial damage — look at *average loss* ($269K) and *dual extortion share* (70%) instead.

The frequency increase (+3%) combined with severity decrease (−19%) suggests the overall insurance market is handling *more* incidents more cheaply per event. This is consistent with broader market-softening documented in [[sources/marsh-global-insurance-market-index-2025]]: improved controls + better IR = lower per-claim cost even as incidents multiply.
