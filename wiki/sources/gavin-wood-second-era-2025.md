# Gavin Wood — Second Era Announcements (Berlin Web3 Summit, July 2025)

At the 2025 Berlin Web3 Summit, Gavin Wood unveiled a package of proposals positioning Polkadot's "Second Era" — a shift from infrastructure-building to product and go-to-market.

**Key source articles:**
- https://medium.com/@polkadot_eri/polkadot-ecosystem-weekly-observations-gavin-unveils-series-of-plans-including-dot-hard-cap-3394c82e811c
- https://www.bitget.com/news/detail/12560605076502

## Announcements

### 1. DOT Hard Cap — [[concepts/dot-hard-cap]]
- Supply cap of **2.1 billion DOT** (originally floated as 3.14B at the summit)
- Enacted via **Referendum 1710** (81% approval) on **March 14, 2026**
- Annual issuance cut by ~53.6%; Bitcoin-style issuance curves going forward
- Rationale: shift DOT's economic model from inflationary to store-of-value

### 2. Proof of Personhood (PoP) — [[concepts/proof-of-personhood]]
- Proposed replacement for current staking mechanism
- Uses on-chain decentralized human validation to distinguish real individuals from bots/AI
- Three-stage rollout:
  - DIM1: Proof of Individuality
  - DIM2: Proof of Verified Individuality
  - DIM3: Further TBD
- Filed as Gavin's first personal treasury proposal; framed as restarting "human-centric growth"

### 3. Security cost reduction
- Validator reward cap: **$5,000/month** with biennial halving
- Projected to cut network security costs **80%**: from ~$500M/year to ~$90M/year
- Makes Polkadot's security budget sustainable long-term relative to network revenue

### 4. pUSD Native Stablecoin
- DOT-collateralized stablecoin deployed as a pallet on Asset Hub
- Deeply integrated with Treasury, Staking, and DeFi systems
- Test version rumored for November 2025

### 5. Polkadot Hub
- Final piece of the "Plaza" plan
- Native smart contract support via the **Revive** project (PolkaVM + EVM interpreter)
- Developers choose: 100% Ethereum compatibility or maximum Polkadot performance
- Part of a broader UX shift: "Platform and Product" phase

## Context: JAM positioning
Wood stated:
> *"After EVM, JAM will become the new industry consensus."*

The summit debates whether to call the JAM transition "Polkadot 3.0" — Wood favors framing it as a foundational system transformation beyond versioning.
