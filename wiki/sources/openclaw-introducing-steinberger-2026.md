# Introducing OpenClaw — Peter Steinberger (2026-01-29)

First-party announcement post on `openclaw.ai/blog/introducing-openclaw` formalizing the rebrand of Steinberger's "WhatsApp Relay" weekend project — through its **Clawd** and **Moltbot** intermediate names — into the final **[[concepts/openclaw|OpenClaw]]** identity.

## Key claims

- **Naming evolution** explicitly retold:
  - *Clawd* (Nov 2025) — pun on "Claude" + "claw"; abandoned after Anthropic's legal concerns.
  - *Moltbot* (Dec 2025) — chosen during a community Discord discussion; lobster-molting metaphor for shedding-to-grow; rejected because it "never quite rolled off the tongue."
  - *OpenClaw* (Jan 2026) — trademark searches completed and domain acquired before the rename.
- **Repository:** `github.com/openclaw/openclaw`.
- **Channels named in the post:** WhatsApp, Telegram, Discord, Slack, Microsoft Teams, Twitch, Google Chat.
  - ⚠️ Signal and iMessage are widely cited in third-party 2026 guides but are **not** named in this first-party post.
- **New in this release:** model support for KIMI K2.5 and Xiaomi MiMo-V2-Flash; web chat interface with image support; 34 security-focused commits in this cycle.
- **Not mentioned in this post:** explicit license string (MIT is asserted elsewhere — repo + community guides), computer-use mechanics, NVIDIA, or [[concepts/nemoclaw]] (the NVIDIA sandbox integration came later in 2026).

## Why it matters for this wiki

This is the canonical "year zero" reference for the OpenClaw name and project history. Anything dated before 2026-01-29 should be referred to by its then-current name (Clawd/Clawdbot or Moltbot) and forward-referenced to OpenClaw via this source.
