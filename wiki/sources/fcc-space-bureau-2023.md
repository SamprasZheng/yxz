# FCC Space Bureau — Creation and Jurisdiction (2023)

## Source

Federal Register notice: `federalregister.gov/documents/2023/04/10/2023-07066/establishment-of-the-space-bureau-and-the-office-of-international-affairs-and-reorganization-of-the`  
FCC document page: `fcc.gov/document/establishment-space-bureau-and-office-international-affairs`  
Launch event announcement: `fcc.gov/document/fcc-space-bureau-office-international-affairs-launches-april-11`  
Official FCC Order adopted: January 4, 2023; released January 9, 2023; effective April 10, 2023

## What Changed

The FCC **reorganised its International Bureau** into two successor bodies:

1. **Space Bureau** — handles all policy and licensing matters related to satellite communications and in-space activities under the Commission's jurisdiction (Part 25 space station and earth station authorizations, small satellite licensing, orbital debris rules, spectrum-use policy for satellite services)
2. **Office of International Affairs** — handles foreign/international regulatory coordination, international telecommunications licensing (Section 214), and submarine cable landing licenses

The reorganisation was triggered by the explosion in commercial satellite activity (Starlink, Kuiper, OneWeb, AST SpaceMobile, etc.) and the FCC's recognition that the International Bureau's dual mandate had grown too large for a single organisation to manage technically.

## Space Bureau Mandate and Divisions

The Space Bureau promotes a **competitive and innovative global communications marketplace** by:
- Leading policy and licensing matters related to satellite and space-based communications
- Authorising satellite space station and earth station systems under Part 25
- Streamlining regulatory processes for commercial space activities
- Coordinating with NASA, DoD, FAA, NOAA, and other federal agencies on space-related technical matters

Internal divisions include:
- **Satellite Programs and Policy Division** — principal licensing and rulemaking unit; handles SAT-LOA, SAT-MOD, and related IBFS processing
- **Spectrum and Competition Policy Division** — spectrum allocation rulemakings

## IBFS Administration After Reorganisation

The Space Bureau directly administers [[sources/fcc-ibfs-portal-2023|IBFS]] for satellite services (SAT-*, SES-*, ELS/*) while the Office of International Affairs handles the Section 214 and submarine cable licensing queues that previously shared the International Bureau's database.

The Space Bureau processes [[concepts/processing-round|processing rounds]] under Part 25, coordinates the ITU filing pipeline (API → CR → N), and enforces EPFD compliance conditions on granted licences.

## Key Rulemakings Post-Reorganisation

Several major satellite rulemakings were processed or initiated by the Space Bureau since its April 2023 launch:
- **SpaceX Gen2 V-band partial grant** (Nov 2024, DA-24-1193): V-band + E-band operations at 340–360 km altitude — the Space Bureau's first major constellation expansion grant
- **AST SpaceMobile SCS authorization** (2024–2025): direct-to-device supplemental coverage from space, coordinated jointly with the Wireless Telecommunications Bureau
- **Space Modernization for the 21st Century NPRM** (FCC 24-97, Dec 2024 / FR Dec 2025): proposed replacing [[concepts/schedule-s|Schedule S]] with Schedule O and Schedule F; expediting initial processing timelines; streamlining small-satellite and experimental licence procedures
- **Expediting Initial Processing NPRM** (FR 2025-08-27): proposed cutting the 6–9 month baseline for uncontested GSO applications to under 6 months

## Relationship to ITU

The Space Bureau is the US Administration's operational contact at the ITU Radiocommunication Bureau (BR) for satellite coordination. It:
- Submits API, CR/C, and Notification filings to the ITU BR on behalf of US-licensed operators
- Forwards ITU coordination letters to operators via IBFS and requests responses within 3–4 business days prior to ITU deadlines
- Maintains the mapping between IBFS file numbers and ITU network names / SNS records

See [[concepts/ngso-gso-coordination]] for the full ITU coordination pipeline.
