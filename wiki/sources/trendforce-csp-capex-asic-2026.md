# TrendForce CSP CapEx + ASIC 2026 — Hyperscaler Custom-Silicon Deployment Report

**Publisher:** TrendForce (market intelligence firm, Taiwan)
**Published:** February 25, 2026
**URL:** [trendforce.com/presscenter/news/20260225-12934.html](https://www.trendforce.com/presscenter/news/20260225-12934.html)
**Companion paid report:** [2026 Cloud AI Outlook: NA Hyperscalers Target GPU & ASICs](https://www.trendforce.com/research/download/RP251105VO)

## Key Statistics (as of publication date)

### Aggregate CSP Spending

- Combined CapEx of the top 8 CSPs (Google/Alphabet, Amazon/AWS, Meta, Microsoft, Oracle, ByteDance, Tencent, Alibaba) projected to exceed **$710 billion in 2026**, representing approximately **61% YoY growth** versus 2025. A separate TrendForce estimate from May 2026 revised the top-9 figure (adding Baidu) up further to ~$830 billion.

### ASIC Penetration Rate

- ASIC-based AI server shipments are projected to reach **27.8% of the total AI server market in 2026** — the highest share since 2023.
- Custom ASIC shipments projected to grow **44.6% year-over-year in 2026**.
- The overall trajectory shows a structural shift from pure GPU dominance toward a GPU + custom-silicon dual architecture.

### Google TPU Leadership

- TPUs (Trillium / v6e + Ironwood / v7) are projected to represent **nearly 78% of AI servers shipped to Google in 2026**.
- Google is described as the **only CSP whose AI server build-out features more ASIC-based servers than GPU-based ones**.
- Alphabet's 2026 CapEx estimated at $178.3 billion (approximately 95% YoY growth).

### Other CSP GPU vs. ASIC Mix (2026 projections)

| CSP | GPU share | ASIC/custom share | CapEx 2026 (est.) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Google/Alphabet | ~22% | ~78% (TPU-led) | ~$178B |
| Amazon/AWS | ~60% | ~40% (Trainium-led) | undisclosed |
| Meta | ~80% | ~20% (MTIA-led) | $125–145B |
| Microsoft | ~85% | ~15% (Maia-led) | ~$190B |

- Amazon's next-generation Trainium 3 ASIC expected to ramp in Q2 2026.

## Context / Caveats

- "ASIC-based AI servers" includes both internally designed accelerators and custom chips from design partners (Broadcom, Marvell). The 27.8% figure spans both categories.
- GPU figures reflect NVIDIA (H100/H200/B200/GB200) plus AMD Instinct; Broadcom/Marvell chips are classified under ASIC.
- No Broadcom or Marvell revenue breakdown appears in this press release; custom-silicon design-partner financials must be sourced from AVGO/MRVL earnings filings.
