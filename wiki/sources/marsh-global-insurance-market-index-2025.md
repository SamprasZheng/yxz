# Marsh Global Insurance Market Index — 2024–2026 Quarterly Cyber Rate Series

## What This Source Is

The Marsh Global Insurance Market Index (GIMI) is a quarterly benchmark of commercial insurance pricing trends published by Marsh, the world's largest insurance brokerage. It surveys renewal data across property, casualty, financial/professional lines (FINPRO), and **cyber** across major geographies. The cyber sub-index is the most widely cited institutional measure of cyber insurance pricing direction.

> **Key takeaway (ingested 2026-06-01):** Cyber insurance rates have declined in every single quarter since at least Q2 2022. The US market specifically has seen 11+ consecutive quarterly rate decreases through Q4 2025. This **soft market persists despite rising attack frequency** — driven by insurer capacity influx and competition, NOT by reduced threat levels.

## Why Rates Are Falling Despite Rising Attacks

Marsh and industry commentary identify four structural drivers of the soft market:

1. **Capital flood:** New entrants and existing carriers expanded cyber capacity significantly 2021–2025, intensifying competition.
2. **Improved controls adoption:** Insurers price favorably for MFA, EDR, network segmentation, and IR planning — pushing rates down for qualified policyholders.
3. **Favorable loss experience (2022–2024):** Ransomware frequency briefly plateaued in 2022–2023 after the LockBit/ALPHV takedowns, allowing carriers to build reserves.
4. **Take-up rate growth:** More companies buying cyber insurance spreads fixed costs across a larger pool, enabling competitive pricing.

The decoupling — **rates falling while attacks rise in 2025** — is the critical interpretation trap for analysts. See [[concepts/cyber-insurance-attack-frequency-indicator]] §Interpretation.
