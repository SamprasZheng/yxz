## Consolidated Verified Data Points (as of June 2026)

**Market share (HBM revenue, estimated H1 2025):**
- SK Hynix: ~57–62% (dominant due to early HBM3e quality lead)
- Samsung: ~22% (recovering after HBM3e qualification delay; passed NVIDIA 12-layer HBM3e Sept 2025)
- Micron: ~21% (fastest grower; 12Hi HBM3e now shipping at scale)

**HBM3e pricing (2026 direction):**
- Samsung and SK Hynix planned ~20% ASP increase for 2026 vs. 2025 pricing
- Primary drivers: NVIDIA H200 China re-export (40K–80K chips), Google TPU v7 (8 stacks/chip), Amazon Trainium3 (4 stacks/chip)
- HBM4 12-layer stack price projected to exceed $600 per stack

**HBM4 production timeline:**
- SK Hynix: HBM4 development complete September 2025; mass-production systems operational; paid samples to NVIDIA December 2025; sales ramp H1 2026; M15X Cheongju first clean room complete May 2026
- Samsung: HBM4 mass production targeted February 2026; passed final internal tests December 2025
- Micron: HBM4 mass production ramp calendar year 2026; HBM4E planned 2027–2028

**2026 HBM revenue mix forecast:**
- ~55% HBM4, ~45% HBM3e (TrendForce)
- HBM4 ramp accelerates from Q3 2026

**CoWoS capacity (TSMC):**
- Current (late 2025): 75,000–80,000 wafers/month; 100% booked
- Target end-2026: 120,000–130,000 wafers/month
- Equipment supplier fulfillment: only ~50% of TSMC expansion orders can be filled on time
- OSAT partner ASE/SPIL (CoWoP): 20,000–25,000 wafers/month by end-2026

**SK Hynix capex signal:**
- Maintains capex at ~mid-30% of sales (3-year moving average)
- 2026 investment "significantly higher"
- M15X Cheongju fab investment: >KRW 20 trillion

## Limitations and Use Notes

- TrendForce HBM pricing figures are secondary-source estimates (citing supply-chain contacts), not audited financial data. Treat as indicative range, not exact.
- Yield rate figures are not published as percentages; TrendForce uses qualitative language ("rapidly improving," "yield advantage") rather than exact numbers. Exact yield data is proprietary to manufacturers.
- CoWoS capacity figures (75K–130K wafers/month) are widely reported and internally consistent across multiple TrendForce and independent sources; treated as high-confidence.
- The "55% HBM4 / 45% HBM3e" 2026 revenue mix is a TrendForce forecast; actual outcome depends on HBM4 yield execution and CoWoS capacity delivery.
