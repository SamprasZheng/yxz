# FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3) Annual Reports — 2024 & 2025

## What This Source Is

The FBI's Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3) publishes an annual report each spring covering cybercrime complaints and financial losses for the prior calendar year. It is the broadest US government statistical measure of cybercrime impact and the canonical government reference for attack frequency and financial damage trends.

**Report landing page (all years):** [ic3.gov — Annual Reports](https://www.ic3.gov/annualreport/reports)

**Direct PDF links:**

- 2025 IC3 Report (covers calendar year 2025): [ic3.gov/AnnualReport/Reports/2025_IC3Report.pdf](https://www.ic3.gov/AnnualReport/Reports/2025_IC3Report.pdf) — published approx. March 2026
- 2024 IC3 Report (covers calendar year 2024): [ic3.gov/AnnualReport/Reports/2024_IC3Report.pdf](https://www.ic3.gov/AnnualReport/Reports/2024_IC3Report.pdf) — published May 13, 2025

**FBI press release (2024 report):** [fbi.gov — FBI Releases Annual Internet Crime Report](https://www.fbi.gov/news/press-releases/fbi-releases-annual-internet-crime-report)

|---|---|

| Total reported losses | $20.877 billion |

## Interpretation Notes

**What IC3 data measures:** Self-reported complaints from US victims. This **undercounts true losses** — FBI estimates less than 15% of cybercrime is reported to law enforcement. IC3 losses are therefore a floor, not a ceiling.

**What IC3 data does NOT capture well:**
- International victims (US-centric reporting bias)
- Ransomware true financial impact (the $32M figure for 2025 ransomware is "direct" only; business interruption, remediation, and reputational costs are 10–100x larger per incident)
- Incidents not reported to the FBI (most corporate incidents go to CISA/insurers first)

**As a leading indicator for cybersecurity demand:** Year-over-year loss increases (+33% in 2024, +26% in 2025) and complaint growth translate into expanded cybersecurity budgets at corporations and government agencies — directly supporting the revenue and pipeline of security vendors. ## Update Cadence

Annual; typically published April–May of the following year (2024 data released May 2025; 2025 data released ~March 2026).
