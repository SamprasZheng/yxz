General-purpose large language models are powerful, but they have two hard limitations when it comes to **deep domain analysis**: knowledge staleness and factual dilution in niche areas. Take the Polkadot ecosystem — key 2024–2026 developments like Agile Coretime, RegionX, and JAM are either unanswerable by general models or pulled from outdated versions.

The solution isn't a bigger model — it's **giving the model a structured, trustworthy external knowledge base** and wrapping it with a persona and task prompt. That's why I build myGPTs.

## The System Behind It: Obsidian Wiki + LLM Ingest Loop

These two GPTs aren't improvised. They share an Obsidian vault as their knowledge backbone:

```
wiki/
├── sources/      one file per source — raw ingested material
├── entities/     people, companies, products
├── concepts/     protocols, architectures, supply chains
└── synthesis/    long-form cross-source analyses for publishing
```

Every time I read new material (podcast, paper, earnings call), I run this loop:

1. Discuss key takeaways with Claude Code
2. Write to `sources/<slug>.md`
3. Update or create related `entities/` and `concepts/` pages (one ingest typically touches 5–15 pages)
4. Update `index.md` and `log.md`

The **text content** of this vault is what gets fed into Jamia and Spacesharks. When a reader asks a cross-source question, the model is retrieving across a hand-built context graph — not guessing from the entire internet.

## Why Not Just Use RAG or an API?

I tried. For a knowledge base maintained by one person, Custom GPT has real advantages:

| Requirement | Custom GPT | Self-hosted RAG |
|-------------|-----------|----------------|
| Zero backend, free to share | ✅ | ❌ need to host vector store |
| Sub-10 MB knowledge corpus | ✅ direct upload | ⚠️ over-engineering |
| Front-end UX (mobile too) | ✅ ChatGPT app | ❌ need to build UI |
| Custom tools (news fetch / price query) | ⚠️ limited actions | ✅ fully controllable |

When knowledge scale or interaction patterns exceed Custom GPT's limits, migrating to RAG/Agent architecture is always an option. **Get the knowledge right first — then worry about the system.**
